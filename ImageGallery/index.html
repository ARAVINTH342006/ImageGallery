Food

  • Energy & nutrients: Provides calories and essential nutrients (carbs, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals) to fuel the body and build/repair tissues
  • Survival span: People can survive weeks without food, but strength, immunity, and mental clarity decline rapidly
  • Physical and mental health: Nourishment prevents malnutrition, supports immune health, and sustains cognitive performance and morale

shelter

  • Protection & survival: Shields us from extreme temperatures, wind, rain, predators, and insects—preventing hypothermia, heat stroke, and injury
  • Psychological stability: Offers safety, privacy, and emotional comfort—reducing stress and supporting mental well-being
  • Foundation for growth: Once basic shelter is secured, individuals can focus on food, water, and eventually higher-level needs (social, esteem, growth)
  • Survival timeline: While food may last weeks, exposure might kill in hours or days, making shelter often equally urgent

Water

  • Hydration & bodily functions : Water makes up ~60–70% of our bodies, vital for digestion, nutrient transport, waste removal, temperature regulation, and cell health
  • Survival window: A person can only survive about 3–5 days without water; mild dehydration quickly impairs thinking and focus
  • Minimum need: About 3 L/day for drinking, and around 50 L/day total is recommended to cover hygiene and sanitation
  • Health & safety: Clean water prevents dangerous diseases—like cholera and dysentery—which affect billions when sanitation is poor
